*1. An inrush current of approximately 9 A will flow for approximately 0.1 ms when the power is turned ON.
*2. Complementary Output
       The complementary output has two output transistors (NPN and PNP) as shown below. These two output transistors
       alternately turn ON and OFF depending on the high or low output signal. When using them, pull up to the positive
       power supply voltage level or pull down to 0 V. The complementary output allows flow-in or flow-out of the output
       current and thus the rising and falling speeds of signals are fast. This allows a long cable distance. They can be
       connected to open-collector input devices (NPN, PNP).

*3. The line driver output is a data transmission circuit compatible with RS-422A and long-distance transmission is
       possible with a twisted-pair cable. (AM26LS31 equivalent)
